Understanding Inflammation Markers and Antibodies

1. IgA ASCA

Anti-Saccharomyces cerevisiae antibodies

Type: Antibody (IgA)

What it measures: Immune response against the yeast Saccharomyces cerevisiae.

Relevance: Often elevated in Crohn's disease. IgA type suggests a mucosal (gut lining) immune reaction.

2. IgG ASCA

Anti-Saccharomyces cerevisiae antibodies

Type: Antibody (IgG)

What it measures: Similar to IgA ASCA, but indicates a systemic immune response.

Relevance: Commonly elevated in Crohn's disease, particularly in aggressive or complicated disease.

3. OmpC

Outer membrane protein C

Type: Antibody against a bacterial protein.

What it measures: Immune response against bacterial components commonly found in intestinal microbiota.

Relevance: Indicates increased gut permeability and bacterial translocation, common in Crohn's disease.

4. CBir1 Fla.

Anti-flagellin antibodies

Type: Antibody against bacterial flagellin, specifically CBir1.

What it measures: Immune reaction against bacterial flagellin proteins (structures bacteria use for movement).

Relevance: Commonly elevated in Crohn's disease; indicates altered immune response to gut bacteria.

5. ANCA

Anti-neutrophil cytoplasmic antibodies

Type: Antibody against neutrophils (a type of white blood cell).

What it measures: Immune response targeting proteins inside neutrophils.

Relevance: Often elevated in ulcerative colitis; helps distinguish from Crohn's disease.

Terminology Clarification

EU (Elisa Units)

Measurement unit representing antibody concentration detected by ELISA (enzyme-linked immunosorbent assay).

Pos.

Indicates a positive result (the antibody is present at elevated levels).

Important Notes

These markers are classes of antibodies produced in mucosal areas, such as the gut lining, indicating local immune activation, often secondary to inflammation.

In short, these markers collectively help clinicians diagnose and differentiate types of inflammatory bowel diseases, specifically Crohn's disease and ulcerative colitis, by indicating distinct immune reactions.
